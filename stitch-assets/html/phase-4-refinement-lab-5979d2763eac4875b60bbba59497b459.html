The empirical evaluation of the competing models indicates that Model B (Control Burden) provides a superior fit to the data (ΔAIC = -6.3, CFI = 0.968).

While Model A accurately captures the main effect of system observability on user trust, it fails to account for the cognitive taxation imposed when high observability is coupled with manual control requirements.

Theoretical Contribution

We propose a refined "Paradox of Observable Control". The results demonstrate a significant negative interaction (β = -0.34, p < .01) between Observability and Burden.

  • When burden is low, observability strictly enhances trust.
  • When burden is high, increased observability actually degrades trust, functioning as a signal of system inadequacy rather than transparency.
